In computer science, the Big O Notation is a mathematical format to express the computational complexity of an algorithm. This allows us to determine the time and space that an algorithm will need as its input changes.

This article will cover some examples of Big O Notation in Python, including the Big O calculations of a single for loop, a nested for loop, and the built-in map iterator. We will also calculate the time complexity of the Minimax Algorithm, which can be used in many backtracking problems (such as Tic Tac Toe). At the end, there is a bonus section that mathematically explains how many game states are possible on a 3x3 Tic Tac Toe board.
